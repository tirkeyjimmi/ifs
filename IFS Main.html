About the Organisation

The Indian Forest Service Benevolent and Welfare Trust is a professional, transparent and nationally committed welfare institution created to support officers, frontline staff and families working in some of the most challenging landscapes of the country. The organisation focuses on long-term resilience, emergency response, structured financial assistance, education, and institutional support.

Officer Stories

Officers strengthened surveillance through technology-enabled patrols, community intelligence networks and coordinated enforcement operations. These efforts significantly reduced wildlife crime and enhanced biodiversity protection across critical tiger landscapes.

Participatory conservation models reduced human-wildlife conflict while improving local livelihoods. Collaborative forest governance strengthened ecological resilience and community trust.

Integrated command systems and community fire brigades enabled rapid containment of large-scale forest fires, protecting biodiversity, wildlife habitats and nearby settlements.

Dedicated teams successfully rescued and rehabilitated injured wildlife, ensuring safe reintroduction into natural habitats while enhancing community awareness and participation.

Governance & Leadership

The Indian Forest Service Benevolent and Welfare operates under a structured governance framework ensuring transparency, accountability, and institutional integrity. The leadership comprises senior forest officers and domain experts committed to welfare, conservation, and service excellence.

Vision

To build a resilient welfare ecosystem that safeguards the dignity, security, and long-term wellbeing of Indian Forest Service officers and their families while strengthening national conservation efforts.

Mission

To provide structured financial assistance, emergency response mechanisms, educational support, and institutional backing to forest personnel, ensuring transparency and measurable impact in every initiative undertaken.
